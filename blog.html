Effective Vocabulary Building

March 15, 2024 By Sarah Martinsen

Learning a few new words every day beats cramming a list once a month.

Use words in short spoken sentences right after you learn them.

Keep a tiny notebook (or phone note) and review five words at night.

Learn words in chunks — collocations and short phrases — not single words.

Associate imagery or a short story with each word to fix it in memory.

Practice with a partner: ask/answer questions using the new words.

Use spaced repetition: review day 1, 3, 7, then 14 for long retention.

Read graded readers and underline words, then look up short example sentences.

Make one weekly goal: use 10 learned words in real conversation.

Celebrate small wins — consistency matters more than speed.
